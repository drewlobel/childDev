CHAPTER 1: THEORIES & RESEARCH METHODS

Q1: What did Plato think were the most important goals of education?

CHAPTER 3: GENETICS, HEREDITY & NEURODEVELOPMENT

Q1: Norm of reaction?
A: Range of possible phenotypes from a single genotype across environments.
Q2: Heterozygous for curly hair means?
A: Two different alleles (one curly, one straight).
Q3: Approximate heritability of IQ?
A: ~50% (0.50), increases with age.
Q4: Cat raised in small cage, few toys ‚Äî brain development?
A: Reduced synaptic density, dendritic branching, cortical thickness; visual & social deficits.
Q5: Factors ruled OUT as cause of autism rise?
A: Vaccines (MMR) and parenting style ‚Äî completely discredited.
Q6: Identical twin correlation > fraternal twin correlation ‚Äî conclusion?
A: Significant genetic component for that trait.
Q7: Myelin sheath important for?
A: Speed and efficiency of neural transmission.
Q8: Genetic material a person inherits?
A: Genotype.
Q9: Visual cortex synapses vs prefrontal cortex ‚Äî timing?
A: Visual cortex peaks earlier (4‚Äë8 months); prefrontal cortex peaks in adolescence.
Q10: Correct interpretation of heritability estimate?
A: Proportion of variance in a trait within a specific population, at a specific time, due to genetic differences. (NOT individual, NOT fixed).
Q11: Trait gotten from parents ‚Äî heritable or inherited?
A: Inherited (passed down), but you cannot tell if heritable (could be environmental).
Q12: Sharing household makes people similar ‚Äî called?
A: Shared environment effects.
Q13: Genes produce trait via reliably present environment ‚Äî concept?
A: Canalized development.
Q14: Genes √ó environment ‚Üí final outcome = ?
A: Phenotype.
